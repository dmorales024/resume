Over the past year, I have experienced significant professional growth and contributed meaningfully to several projects, most notably the Automated People Counter (APC) initiative and the AppClip ticketing proof-of-concept. My journey began with what I consider my "big break": successfully resolving the MyID timeout issue, enabling users to save their information in the FIGARO application reliably. I learned the importance of writing reusable code and leveraging best practices, including effective caching techniques and session storage. This foundational lesson led to a major refactor and set the tone for future code improvements across our repositories, decreasing tech debt. Solving the site's timeout issue was a significant bug fix for the business team, as it meant the information they entered in the entire form persisted across sessions, saving them much time.
 
Building on this momentum, I spearheaded multiple refactors within the Figaro application, culminating in the release of Figaro 2.0 for the Minnie Van team. These efforts not only improved code quality but also fostered a culture of maintainable, well-documented, and DRY code. As our team expanded to include three offshore developers, I honed my skills as a PR reviewer, ensuring the team's principles, such as using static constants, eliminating redundant code, and maintaining clear documentation, were consistently applied.
 
In late April, I embarked on the APC project, which quickly became a highlight of my year. I architected a Spring Boot Java service to interface with APS sensors via a SOAP-based protocol, learned Liquibase for database management (a first for the organization outside the database team), and delved into the complexities of secure networking, including mTLS for device-to-service communication. Developing this project from scratch was both challenging and rewarding, especially when my code was reviewed and found to be clear and understandable. The APC system now provides critical ridership data (which they did not have before), empowering bus route planners to optimize operations and enhance the Guest experience: a direct impact on both efficiency and satisfaction. Access to the counts also opens the potential for future integrations with machine learning to optimize bus route scheduling, as they would have more concrete data on how many Guests to expect at a given bus stop at any given time.
 
In parallel with my work on the APC service, I collaborated with the ticketing team to develop an AppClip for media transfer. It was an invaluable learning experience in the Apple ecosystem. I created a Swift-based AppClip that allowed Guests to download their MagicMobile passes directly to their phones, bypassing the OneID JWT. This solution addressed a real-world problem: Guests often mistook emailed barcodes for park entry, so the AppClip I created would enable Cast Members to quickly convert barcode media into a usable format for park entry.
 
I was also given the opportunity to join a team of developers to present to Scott the use cases of AI in our daily SDLC. This initiative is still in its early stages, but I am excited to be part of a group actively shaping the narrative on how AI can be leveraged to enhance our workflows and drive innovation across Disney. By sharing my knowledge of machine learning and AI tools, I am helping our team identify practical applications from automating code reviews and improving test coverage to streamlining documentation and accelerating feature development. Our goal is to demonstrate how responsible and strategic adoption of AI can reduce manual effort, increase productivity, and foster a culture of continuous improvement.
 
Beyond these major projects, I continued to expand my network within Disney, connecting with leaders from Connected Products and Emerging Tech to explore new opportunities and innovations, such as Sarg+ and advancements in the T&L lab. Outside of work, I am preparing to apply to a master's program in ML and AI, developing video games in Godot to build my own arcade cabinet, and mentoring a FIRST Tech Challenge robotics team. I also pursue engineering projects in CAD (Onshape), blending creativity with technical skill.
 
Reflecting on the year, the APC and AppClip projects stand out as milestones that not only advanced my technical abilities but also deepened my understanding of how thoughtful engineering can directly improve user experiences. I have consistently done the right things to make the most of the opportunities presented by the Agile approach, despite the limited capital we have. These accomplishments, supported by a foundation of best practices and collaborative growth, have positioned me to take on even greater challenges in the future.
